Online Consent Form

This task is part of a research study conducted by Professor Emma Brunskill at Carnegie Mellon University and is funded by Carnegie Mellon University and Microsoft Corporation gift money. The purpose of the research is to explore how to combine crowdsourcing and machine learning to make learning more efficient, engaging and effective.

Procedures

If you choose to participate, you will be asked to be either a tutor or a learner. As a learner, you will be asked to learn a topic of interest or a given topic in our system. We will assess the learning output, for example, by pretests and posttests. As a tutor, you will be asked to give help to the learner by interacting with the learner (such as gathering materials, creating questions etc.). No prior knowledge about any topics is assumed, for either role. The duration of the study will be at least 1 minute and no more than 10 hours. You may quit after any activity.

Participant Requirements

Participation in this study is limited to individuals age 18 and older.

Risks

The risks and discomfort associated with participation in this study are no greater than those ordinarily encountered in daily life, educational institutions, or during other online activities. As with normal tasks you perform in your normal day-to-day life, you may become bored or tired completing our tasks. There is also a risk of confidentiality being breached.

Benefits

You may learn something of interest to you during this study, or there may no personal benefit to you from participation, but the knowledge received may be of value to humanity.

Compensation & Costs

You will receive $1 as the base for participation. You may receive up to $4 as the extra bonus which depends on your performance.

Confidentiality

By participating in this research, you understand and agree that Carnegie Mellon may be required to disclose your consent form, data and other personally identifiable information as required by law, regulation, subpoena or court order. Otherwise, your confidentiality will be maintained in the following manner: Your data and consent form will be kept separate. Your consent form will be stored in a locked location on Carnegie Mellon property and will not be disclosed to third parties. By participating, you understand and agree that the data and information gathered during this study may be used by Carnegie Mellon and published and/or disclosed by Carnegie Mellon to others outside of Carnegie Mellon. However, your name, address, contact information and other direct personal identifiers in your consent form will not be mentioned in any such publication or dissemination of the research data and/or results by Carnegie Mellon.
